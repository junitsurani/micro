5. Brand Voice & Copy Direction
Tone
Whisper, not sell. Warm minimalism. Intelligent calm. Short sentences. Intentional rhythm.
Statements that land like exhales. The reader should think: “that’s exactly what I’ve been
feeling.”
Language Lines (approved)
• Created with intention
• Transformation begins within
• Stillness with a pulse
• More life. Not just more years.
• Internal before external
• Energy, contained
• Precision is a form of care
• Movement held in restraint
• The system beneath the surface
• Felt before seen
• You fix you
• Every movement is a Fix
• Not a workout. A recalibration.
Words to Use
Restore, realign, recalibrate, ground, ignite, sculpt, awaken, soften, strengthen, release, fix,
return, rise, still, shift, move, breathe, build, surrender, hold. Intentional, calibrated, quiet, raw,
grounded, elevated, slow, controlled, sensory, architectural.
Words to Avoid
Hustle, grind, smash, burn, shred, beast mode, no pain no gain, vibes, basic, affordable,
discount, cheap. Never call it a “wellness studio” or categorise it. Never code female. Never
code male. Just code intentional.
Copy Checklist
• Does it sound like MICRO — or like any other studio?
• Would I whisper this — or shout it?
• Is there breath in this sentence?
• Does it respect the intelligence of the reader?
• Does it make someone feel something before it explains anything?
Location Language
Always “Chelsea”, never “London.” Chelsea is specific, aspirational, signals the right world.
6. Visual & Motion Direction
Hero
The hero is the first breath. Warm atmospheric gradient (see colour system), the terrain mark
animation or hero video loop, and a single line of text in FreightBig Pro Light Italic. No CTA in the
hero. Let the visitor arrive.
Photography Direction
Architectural, not editorial. Bodies in space, not bodies on display. Light and shadow over posed
shots. Texture and materiality over faces. The space is the subject. When people appear, they
are in the space — not performing for a camera.
Note: Studio photography is not yet available (pre-construction). Use atmospheric/textural
imagery and the hero video/animation as primary visual assets for launch site.
Motion Principles
• Controlled pacing — nothing fast, nothing abrupt
• Large negative space — let elements breathe
• Reduced visual density — less on screen at any moment
• Subtle movement behaviour — parallax, fade, atmospheric transitions
Terrain Mark
The terrain mark is the brand’s secondary icon — it represents internal topography: depth,
fascia, rhythm, invisible structure. It does not illustrate energy. It organises it. Use at digital scale
(logo) and micro scale (favicon).

9. Creative Direction Summary
Build a website that feels like the first breath you take when you walk into a space
that was made for you.
This is not a website that sells. It is a website that makes someone feel something — and then
gives them exactly one thing to do: join the waitlist.
Every scroll should feel deliberate. Every transition should feel like a shift in state. The negative
space is not empty — it’s doing the same job as the silence between notes in music. The
typography should breathe. The colour should warm. The motion should calm.
The visitor should leave the site and not be able to fully explain what they just experienced —
but they should feel compelled to fill in the form. That’s the subconscious fix, working through a
screen.
Make it quiet enough to hear. Make it warm enough to feel. Make it precise enough to trust.
